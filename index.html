About Me

I am a web developer who loves researching, problem-solving and visually creative input and output in a variety of mediums.

I started coding in the mid 80s with my Texas Instrument TI-99 console. After I got my fill of A-Maze-ing, Alpiner and Wumpus, I cracked open the user manual and found tons of coding projects. It took hours of entering lines of BASIC to make a 16-bit Mr. Bojangles dance. It was a trip to show my 10 year-old son video clips of the games I played on my TI-99 when I was 10.

Over the years, I've enjoyed punching-up Ebay listings for family members and customizing code for small businesses via their preferred web design platforms. I may even be known to have replaced a motherboard once upon a time. I still enjoy taking things apart.

I’m currently expanding and enhancing my skills through the GW Full-Stack Coding Bootcamp.

When I’m not in front of my computer, I love exploring new hiking trails, getting grounded on my yoga mat, escaping into a mystery novel, sampling new recipes in the kitchen and figuring out ways to make my kids laugh.
